CEED — Common Entrance Exam for Design

CEED — the Common Entrance Examination for Design — is conducted by IIT Bombay and opens pathways to M.Des programmes at IITs, IISc Bangalore, and other top design institutions across India. It is the benchmark exam for design graduates who want to take their career to the next level.

Unlike undergraduate design exams, CEED expects a mature understanding of design — testing sketching, visual communication, creative problem-solving, and design thinking at a much deeper level. The competition is intense, and without structured CEED entrance exam preparation in Pune, even talented designers can fall short.

Part-A Syllabus
CBT
Visualization & Spatial Reasoning
Understanding and transformation of 2D and 3D shapes, spatial relationships
Scientific & Practical Knowledge
Understanding of everyday scientific concepts and objects
Observation & Design Sensitivity
Attention to detail, ability to detect hidden patterns, and critical analysis
Environment & Society
Awareness of environmental, social, and cultural connections in design
Analytical & Logical Reasoning
Interpretation of data and logical thinking
Language
Proficiency in reading and understanding Standard English
Creativity
Interpretation of analogies, metaphors, symbols (verbal and visual)
Art & Design Knowledge
Familiarity with art/design history, artists, products, and current trends
Design Methods & Practices
Understanding of materials, media, production methods, and ergonomics

Pen & Paper
Drawing
Freehand drawing of scenes, products, and people with correct proportion, perspective, shading
Creativity
Ability to think divergently and present original ideas and solutions
Communication
Clarity in expressing ideas visually and through written content
Problem Identification
Understanding user needs, appropriate use of materials, and design context
